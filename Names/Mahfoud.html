Nadjib Mahfoud


Mahfoud in 1980s.

Nadjib Mahfoud is an Egyptian writer who won the 1988 Nobel Prize in Literature. Mahfoud is regarded as one of the first contemporary writers in Arabic literature, along with Taha Hussein, to explore themes of existentialism. All of his novels take place in Egypt, and always mentions the lane, which equals the world. His most famous works include The Cairo Trilogy and Children of Gebelawi. While Mahfoud's literature is classified as realist literature, existential themes appear in it.
